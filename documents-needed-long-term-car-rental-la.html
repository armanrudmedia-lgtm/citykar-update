What Documents Do I Need to Rent a Car Long Term in Los Angeles?

Renting a car long term in Los Angeles requires specific documentation including a valid driver's license, proof of insurance or insurance coverage through the rental company, and meeting age and deposit requirements. This guide clarifies what you need to know when renting from City Kar.

To rent a car long term in Los Angeles with City Kar, you generally need a valid driver's license, a major payment card for deposits and fees, proof of insurance (or you may purchase coverage through City Kar), and must meet age requirements (typically 21 or older, with possible surcharges under 25). Additional documentation or deposits may apply depending on your situation. Always confirm current terms with City Kar before booking.

If you're planning to rent a hybrid car long term in Los Angeles, knowing the documentation and eligibility requirements upfront can save time and prevent surprises. City Kar specializes in long-term hybrid rentals designed for residents and visitors alike, offering a practical alternative to ownership. Understanding Eligibility and Documentation for Long-Term Rentals

Driver's License

A valid driver's license is mandatory. For City Kar rentals in Los Angeles, this means a government-issued license valid for the duration of your rental. International visitors may need an International Driving Permit alongside their home country license. The license should be free of major restrictions or suspensions.

Age Requirements and Fees

Most rental companies, including City Kar, require renters to be at least 21 years old. Renters under 25 often face a "young driver surcharge" due to increased insurance risk, as noted by AAA [3]. Some exceptions or additional documentation may be needed for drivers aged 18–20, but these are rare and subject to strict terms.

Insurance and Financial Responsibility

California law requires all vehicles operated on public roads to have minimum liability insurance coverage: at least $30,000 for injury/death of one person, $60,000 for injury/death of more than one person, and $15,000 for property damage [1]. City Kar provides insurance options that meet or exceed these minimums, but renters should confirm coverage details and whether their personal insurance or credit card benefits apply.

Deposit and Payment Methods

Long-term rentals typically require a security deposit held on a major credit or debit card. The deposit amount varies and is refundable upon vehicle return without damage or excess mileage. City Kar accepts standard payment cards but advises confirming exact deposit policies at booking.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I rent a car long term in Los Angeles if I'm under 25?
Most rental companies, including City Kar, allow renters aged 21–24 but usually apply a young driver surcharge. Renters under 21 are rarely accepted for long-term rentals. Confirm specific age policies and fees with City Kar.
Do I need my own car insurance to rent from City Kar?
Renters must have proof of financial responsibility as required by California law [1]. City Kar offers insurance coverage options that meet these requirements if you do not have personal insurance that covers rentals. Always review your coverage options before renting.
What forms of payment does City Kar accept for deposits and rental fees?
City Kar generally accepts major credit and debit cards for deposits and payments. The deposit amount depends on rental duration and vehicle type. Confirm payment methods and deposit amounts directly with City Kar.
What happens if I don't have a California driver's license?
Visitors with out-of-state or international licenses can rent if their license is valid and recognized. Some may need an International Driving Permit. Confirm eligibility with City Kar before booking.
Are additional documents like proof of residence or employment required?
For long-term rentals, some companies may request additional verification such as proof of residence or employment. City Kar's requirements can vary, so verify specific documentation needs when arranging your rental.
