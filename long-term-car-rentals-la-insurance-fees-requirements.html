Do Long-Term Car Rentals in Los Angeles Include Insurance?

If you're considering a long-term car rental in Los Angeles, it's important to know how insurance, deposits, and driver requirements work. This guide clarifies what to expect from companies like City Kar, especially regarding insurance coverage and eligibility for hybrid rentals.

When renting a car for an extended period in Los Angeles, a common question is whether insurance is included in the rental agreement. The short answer is: most long-term rental companies, including City Kar, provide basic insurance coverage as part of the rental, but drivers should carefully review the terms and consider additional coverage options. Understanding eligibility requirements, deposits, and documentation will help you make an informed decision and avoid surprises.

Insurance Coverage in Long-Term Rentals: What to Expect

California law requires all vehicles operated on public roads to have minimum liability insurance coverage. The minimum financial responsibility limits are $30,000 for injury or death to one person, $60,000 for injury or death to more than one person, and $15,000 for property damage [1]. Rental companies ensure their fleet meets these requirements, but the extent of coverage provided to renters can vary.

City Kar's long-term hybrid rentals include insurance coverage that meets California's legal minimums. However, renters should confirm current terms with City Kar, as additional insurance options—such as collision damage waivers or personal injury protection—may be available or recommended depending on your needs.

Eligibility, Deposits, and Documentation

Renting a car long-term in Los Angeles involves meeting certain eligibility criteria and providing documentation upfront. Here's what you generally need to know:

  • Age Requirements: Most rental companies require renters to be at least 21 years old. Drivers under 25 may face additional fees or restrictions [2]. Confirm City Kar's specific age policies before booking.
  • Driver's License: A valid driver's license is mandatory. International visitors should check if an International Driving Permit (IDP) or other documentation is required.
  • Deposit: Long-term rentals often require a security deposit. The amount varies by company and vehicle type.
  • Payment Method: A credit card is typically required for the deposit and rental payments.
  • Insurance Proof: If you have personal auto insurance or coverage through a credit card, bring proof. City Kar can advise on how your existing insurance interacts with their coverage.
